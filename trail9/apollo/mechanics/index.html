Mechanics

Subtle interaction motifs

Interactive elements in Apollo utilize fluid motions that give a sense of weightlessness to actions and transitions. Even in data-heavy environments, we aim for clear interfaces that inform and delight the user.

Focal Depth

Elements along the z-axis use shortened focal depth to draw attention to the object in the foreground. Elements in the background may or may not have a subtle blur eﬀect.

Material

The core material feels like it is in a 3D environment. It ﬂoats ﬂuidly along, x, y, and z-axis. Objects should have a sense of weightlessness and should ﬂoat through space with zero friction.

Reverb

Short, light, quick ripples from a circular shape convey a signal sent or a reaction that is about to take place such as launching a menu overlay.

  • Line weight 2px
  • Ripple circle: 120%
  • Ripple line: 1px, 70% opacity

Position & Rotation

Elements that change position, transform or rotate should use a curve that eases in, speeds up a little, and slows down.

  • There should never be any bounce at the end

Scale & Transform

Elements that scale up, down, or transform should use a curve that eases out.

  • There should never be any bounce at the end

Translucency

To reinforce the weightless quality of elements, some items closer to the camera/on top may have a translucent quality to them. The recommendation is to apply a just enough opacity so that when transitions occur, it has a more dramatic eﬀect.

  • Tile elements are 80%-90% opacity
  • We see reﬂected light and shadow cast from it
